My Early Life

I grew up in San Diego, California, where I spent a lot of my early years between a soccer field and a piano bench. I started playing piano around age eight, and over seven years it became one of the most consistent parts of my life, taking me through recitals, competitions, and more practice hours than I can count. Soccer was just as central; I played left winger for years and developed a competitive instinct that has carried into everything I do now. Outside of sports and music, I was always drawn to how things worked at a larger scale, why economies move, why some businesses survive and others do not, and why global events unfold the way they do. San Diego gave me a lot of room to explore those interests, and I started building toward them earlier than most people realize. Looking back, the discipline I developed through piano and athletics set the foundation for how I approach every serious pursuit in my life today. Those early years were less about achievement and more about developing the habits that make achievement possible.

My Current Interests

Right now, I am most focused on economics, entrepreneurship, international relations, and the intersection of all three. I co-founded and currently serve as Co-President of the CCA Economics Club, where I oversee financial literacy outreach to local schools and lead our participation in national competitions like the National Economics Challenge and the Wharton Global High School Investment Competition. On the entrepreneurship side, I run Arbora, a handcrafted moss terrarium brand I developed through my Junior Achievement coursework, where I operate as CFO and manage everything from sales strategy to vendor outreach. Public speaking and leadership are central to both roles; I have learned that the ability to persuade a room and move a team toward a goal is one of the most transferable skills you can build. I am also deeply interested in how global policy and economics interact, which shapes the way I read the news and how I think about my academic future. Piano remains a constant in the background, separate from career-building but no less important to who I am. Every one of these interests feeds into a larger picture of the kind of person and professional I am working to become.

My Future Plans

My long-term goal is to study international relations with a strong foundation in economics and business at a top university, then build a career at the intersection of global affairs, policy, and markets. I am drawn to fields like international finance, trade policy, and diplomacy, where understanding how capital and power interact on a global scale is the core of the work. Everything I am doing now, from leading the Economics Club to running Arbora to developing as a public speaker, is part of a deliberate effort to build the skills and record that a path like that demands. I think about my high school years the way a founder thinks about early-stage company building: with a clear thesis and a plan to prove it before anyone asks me to. I do not treat my extracurriculars as resume decoration; I treat them as early-stage proof of concept for the kind of leader and thinker I intend to be. The goal is not just admission to a great school but a life built around the problems I actually want to solve. I am exactly where I need to be, and I am just getting started.
